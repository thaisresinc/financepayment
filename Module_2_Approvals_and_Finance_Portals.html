WATCH THIS
If an install is delayed past the expiry date, the approval lapses. For Parker Lane and Brighte that usually just means resubmitting the application — annoying, but fixable. For Plenti, there is no fix: the application must be cancelled and a completely new one created from scratch.
GETTING THE NUMBERS RIGHT: DEPOSIT AMOUNT
Getting the numbers right: deposit amount
“Deposit amount” in Zoho means any money the customer is paying in cash that isn't covered by finance — it is NOT a deposit figure plus a separate cash figure added together. For a mixed finance-and-cash order, this field must reflect the FULL cash portion due on completion, not just whatever smaller amount the customer chose to pay upfront as an early gesture. This is one of the most common data-entry mistakes reps make: they enter the small early part-payment instead of the full cash portion still owing. Always check the finance notes on the order — they should state clearly whether some of the cash portion is due before install, or whether all of it is due on completion.

BRIGHTE — THE DISCOUNT RULE YOU CANNOT GET WRONG
Brighte — the discount rule you cannot get wrong
Two products, opposite rules on discounting.
Standard Brighte
Merchant fee applies
No discount allowed under any circumstances — the merchant fee already consumes the margin. Discounting on top of this comes straight out of RESINC's pocket.
Interest-free loan (NSW)
No merchant fee
Discount is allowed, provided the financed amount stays under the $15,000 cap. Above that cap, the interest-free product no longer applies and standard rules kick back in.
